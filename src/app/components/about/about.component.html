About Us

StoryTracker, the internet's only 100% free-as-in-beer story submission and reader tracking website!

Who are we?

"We" are Damon Kaswell. I'm a writer and developer (not necessarily in that order) located in Eugene, Oregon. I'm an alumni of the Wordos writing workshop, and prior 3rd-place winner of the Writers of the Future contest. I have multiple publishing credits, and while my focus has shifted from writing stories to writing code, I have a great deal of love for both.

Why are you doing this?

There are a number of different ways a writer can keep track of the cloud of metadata surrounding her fiction:

  • Try to memorize every single detail. Word count? Submission history? First readers? Just memorize it! (Note: This never works.)
  • Keep everything in a text file that can't be sorted and will inevitably be lost or become extremely cumbersome to use. (Note: This actually works, but is universally hated.)
  • Spend a year trying to master spreadsheets well enough to be almost sure, kinda, that the macro she wrote to record market submissions will do it correctly. (Note: Yeah, right.)
  • Design a personal database using something like Microsoft Access. (Note: No one has ever successfully done this.)
  • Sign up for a web-based service. (Note: This is the only choice that reliably works.)

There are several quality services that do the job well. But none of them do quite the job I want. Their focus tends to be on tracking markets, with submissions as an afterthought, and nothing to take reader notes on. Having come from a major writing group, I want some way to take notes during a critique session and have them associated with my story. Nothing quite works for that right now.

So one day, I thought to myself, "Damon, you're a developer. Why don't you create this?" And I did.

Is this really free?

Yes. Really free. There will never be a fee, and there will never be a "pro" or "premium" version. It might get advertising, and you can donate to me to help keep the server running if you want. But this is a labor of love for the science fiction and fantasy writing community (and a lovely addition to my development portfolio; I personally wrote every single line of code for this project).

Neat! Can I help?

Sure! The code is open source, divided into two pieces:

  • Frontend
  • Backend

The frontend component is written as an Angular application, and uses Bootstrap throughout, because I'm not a graphic designer and might as well be color blind. The backend component is written as a Node.js/Express application, with MongoDB for database storage. If you're inclined to help, go ahead and make that pull request!

This seems a bit rough around the edges.

That's because I'm a one-man show with other responsibilities. Sorry. But I promise your data is safe! And I'm continuously working to improve everything. Thanks for noticing!
